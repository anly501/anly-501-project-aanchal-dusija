Using Data Science in the E-commerce Retail Industry

E-commerce is the buying and selling of goods and services through the internet. It is one of the booming industries in fashion and apparel. E-commerce has played an important role in the fashion and apparel industry. The statistics, trends, and strategies are shaping online sales. E-commerce allows customers to choose a product or service they want from any supplier anywhere in the world right from their fingertips.

The global fashion industry is continuously moving towards a decisive phase of digital adoption by the mainstream consumer, with online sales being projected to grow rapidly, especially in emerging markets. The ease of use of various digital instruments and content has amended the consumer purchase model from a traditional one to a more complex one across both offline and online mediums. Regardless of the chosen medium, consumers expect a consistent brand experience at all times. As such, it is essential that brands adopt omnichannel retailing in their business model, which allows retailers to understand and leverage the interconnectivity between physical and digital mediums.

In many markets, consumers look to online platforms as their first point of reference, as they offer a wider variety of products, and generally provide superior customer relevance and convenience, including insight-driven marketing to seamless customer care and logistics. Due to the numerous opportunities which eCommerce provides to the fashion industry, companies are becoming increasingly able to expand into untapped markets, increase their customer base, and ultimately increase revenues.

Every time you use your mouse to make a purchase in today's world of data from numerous sources, a data trail is recorded and kept and will later be used by shops to entice you to make other purchases. For instance, if you are a customer looking to purchase a new phone, mobile websites or apps may have information about the products you have viewed, Google may know the products you have searched for, and GSMArena (a well-known website for smartphone reviews) may be aware of the mobile phones you have read reviews on. Additionally, you tweeted or posted updates on Facebook with these reviews. E-commerce enterprises may learn what customers want and when they want it by organizing millions of Tweets, Facebook likes Instagram photos and Pinterest photos. Data science includes the process of gathering, collecting, sorting, and analyzing data in order to generate actionable insights. Data science is also used to anticipate a product's lifespan on a website and inform clients of how likely it is that the items will run out soon. This aids the company in forecasting, which is essential for any business and involves determining how many outfits to make and send to a specific market. Big fashion companies will have information on their sales both online and in physical locations, and this information may be utilized to forecast product demand, calculate stock requirements, and set prices. Data Science in eCommerce assists businesses in providing a richer understanding of their customers by capturing and integrating information on their web behavior, events in their lives, what led to the purchase of a product or service, how customers interact with different channels, and so on.

These trends show the rising boom for the e-commerce industry and data science holds the promise of enhancing the shopping behavior of customers that can provide e-commerce businesses with an improved marketing mix and enhanced profitability.
